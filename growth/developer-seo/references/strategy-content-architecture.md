# Strategy, Content, and Information Architecture

Use this reference for opportunity research, intent mapping, page design, content systems, internal linking, and vertical search.

## 1. Start with the market

Define:

- audiences and their recurring jobs, anxieties, constraints, and vocabulary;
- the product's differentiated value and proof;
- high-value actions and economic limits;
- markets, languages, seasonality, regulation, and sales cycle;
- existing assets: experts, data, tools, research, community, inventory, locations, media, and relationships.

SEO strategy chooses where the organization can become the best answer. It is not a spreadsheet of every query with volume.

## 2. Research demand and SERPs

Use first-party query data, site search, sales/support language, interviews, forums, competitor coverage, autocomplete/trends, and paid-search learnings when available. Cluster terms by underlying task, not shared tokens alone.

For representative queries record:

- dominant intent: learn, compare, buy/do, navigate, local, troubleshoot, or mixed;
- result types: category, product, guide, forum, video, local pack, image, news, calculator, AI answer;
- freshness, locality, media, and authority expectations;
- common subquestions and decision criteria;
- whether the business can supply a materially better answer;
- likely conversion path and value.

Search volume is directional and tool-dependent. Preserve source, market, date, and range. Do not fabricate precision.

## 3. Build an opportunity map

Map each intent cluster to one primary page responsibility:

| Intent | Best-fit page | Required proof |
|---|---|---|
| Category discovery | Hub/category | Coverage, filters, differentiation |
| Product/service decision | Product/service | Specifications, availability, evidence, trust |
| Comparison | Comparison/tool | Transparent criteria, alternatives, first-hand data |
| How-to/problem | Guide/tool | Complete steps, examples, outcomes, caveats |
| Local action | Location/service-area | Real presence, details, reviews, route/contact |
| Entity/navigation | About/person/brand | Identity, credentials, relationships, official links |

Consolidate overlapping pages when one stronger answer serves the same task. Keep separate pages when intent, audience, product, geography, or SERP format genuinely differs.

Prioritize with business value, achievable relevance, authority gap, content advantage, technical readiness, and time to evidence—not volume alone.

## 4. Design information architecture

- Organize around user mental models and durable entities, not a temporary keyword list.
- Give major tasks clear hubs and coherent child pages.
- Keep depth proportional to complexity; avoid both flat link dumps and buried orphan pages.
- Use URL paths that are stable, readable, and decoupled from presentation details likely to change.
- Make filters useful to people while controlling which combinations become crawlable/indexable landing pages.
- Connect related pages contextually: category -> item, guide -> solution, entity -> evidence, location -> service.
- Maintain breadcrumbs that reflect a defensible hierarchy.

Document page ownership. Every indexable template needs an intent, audience, unique value, canonical policy, and lifecycle owner.

## 5. Specify the page before writing

Create a brief containing:

- primary user task and stage;
- query/intent cluster and competing result formats;
- page promise and differentiated angle;
- required entities, questions, comparisons, examples, media, and proof;
- first-hand inputs: test, interview, data, experience, artifact, or expert review;
- conversion and next logical action;
- internal links in and out;
- metadata and structured-data eligibility;
- freshness trigger and owner;
- claims requiring sources, legal review, or disclosure.

Do not prescribe a magic word count or keyword density. Cover the task completely with the least content that does so well.

## 6. On-page design

- Write a unique, accurate title that distinguishes the page and earns the right click. Do not force boilerplate ahead of the useful phrase.
- Use a clear primary heading and a scannable structure aligned to the user's decision sequence.
- Answer the core question early when appropriate, then provide depth, evidence, exceptions, and next actions.
- Use the vocabulary users employ while preserving technical accuracy and natural language.
- Make important facts textual even when also shown in images, video, or interactive UI.
- Place comparison criteria in tables only when a table improves comprehension.
- Use descriptive internal anchors without templated keyword stuffing.
- Write meta descriptions as truthful snippet candidates; search engines may select other text.
- Treat FAQs as user support, not a rich-result hack.

## 7. Demonstrate experience and trust

Make it easy to answer:

- Who created or reviewed this?
- What direct experience, method, or data supports it?
- Why does this organization know the topic?
- When was it last materially reviewed?
- What limitations, conflicts, and alternatives exist?
- How can a reader verify important claims?

Use bylines and author pages where expected. Show methodologies, test conditions, photographs, examples, datasets, citations, editorial policies, corrections, and contact/business information when relevant. For YMYL topics, increase expert review, sourcing, safety framing, and update rigor.

## 8. AI-assisted content

Use AI to accelerate research organization, clustering, outlining, transformations, QA, and gap discovery. Keep humans accountable for purpose, factual correctness, originality, judgment, and publication.

For scaled generation require:

- a defensible data or utility advantage;
- explicit source provenance and rights;
- template-level uniqueness beyond token substitution;
- factual and policy validation;
- duplicate/thin-content thresholds;
- sampling and exception queues;
- indexation gates and kill switches;
- named ownership and update rules.

Reject publishing because generation is cheap. The cost of crawling, reviewing, maintaining, and reputational failure remains real.

## 9. Programmatic SEO

Approve an indexable page class only if combinations represent real demand and each page helps users complete a task. Distinguish:

- useful inventory/location/data pages with meaningful unique attributes;
- empty, near-empty, expired, or nonsensical combinations;
- internal search results and arbitrary facets;
- templated doorway pages that funnel to the same destination.

Define minimum viable content/availability, canonical behavior, empty-state status, pagination, crawl limits, and sitemap inclusion before generation. Measure indexed-to-valuable ratios and prune or noindex low-value classes deliberately.

## 10. Content lifecycle

Audit by page and template using impressions, clicks, conversions, backlinks/citations, accuracy, uniqueness, and maintenance cost. Choose one action:

- keep;
- improve;
- consolidate and redirect;
- update with substantive change;
- noindex but retain for users;
- retire with `404`/`410` or relevant redirect.

Do not change dates without meaningful revision. Do not delete content only to manufacture freshness. Preserve external equity and user bookmarks when consolidating.

## 11. Vertical considerations

### Ecommerce

Keep product identity, variants, price, availability, reviews, merchant feeds, canonicals, and category links consistent. Handle out-of-stock and discontinued items based on expected return, alternatives, demand, and backlinks. Prevent filter/sort explosion while retaining valuable category combinations.

### SaaS and services

Connect problem education to capability, use case, comparison, integration, documentation, and proof. Avoid hundreds of thin industry/location permutations. Let product documentation and free tools earn discovery and links.

### Publishers

Prioritize editorial authority, author/topic hubs, original reporting, transparent corrections, news eligibility, and archive architecture. Manage syndicated copies and update timestamps honestly.

### Local

Build real location/service utility: hours, access, staff, inventory/services, local proof, photos, FAQs, and contact paths. Avoid virtual-office and city-keyword doorway patterns.

### Images, video, and interactive tools

Choose the format users and SERPs reward. Pair media/tools with indexable explanatory context, stable landing URLs, metadata, transcripts/captions where appropriate, and clear sharing/embed value.
